FAQ

Do you really think that idea could work?

We really do. Because we believe that the amount of decent honest people in the world is way higher then the minimum required for something like this to work.
Yeah, we'll keep on staying foolish, just like uncle Steve taught us.
Have you heard of the Agile Manifesto? FreedomSponsors is all about taking the third principle to its ultimate conseqences:

  • "Customer collaboration over contract negotiation".
We think this tool we're providing will give people the ability to give the right push to open source projects, so that things go faster; while also enabling programmers to do more work on the projects they love.

How do I change my profile picture?

We don't store images in our database.
Instead we pull user pictures from Gravatar.
Gravatar is a nice and simple online service that will let you upload avatar pictures and associate them with your email(s). Here's an example:

  • email - tony@freedomsponsors.org
  • picture URL - http://www.gravatar.com/avatar/b2f9b553e184602579b920053952ce4c.png?s=160
Please go ahead, and create your Gravatar account, and associate an avatar with your primary email here on FS.

How can I tell if a "sponsor" is serious about his offer?

You can't. But you can take a look at his/her history. Everything you do at FreedomSponsors is public.
Check out their profile. If there are a lot of unpaid offers on resolved issues, that's a bad sign.

So you have a reputation system?

Not yet. We just display the users history attached to their profiles.
But we intend to have something like that in the future.

How do I add "credits" to FreedomSponsors?

YOU DON'T!
Payments are made directly from sponsors to developers.
Our wish is that sponsors pay the developers not because they have to, but because they feel grateful for their work.
For that reason, there is nothing on the system that forces nobody to pay anything. There are no escrows and no pre paid credits - this is not an implementation detail - it's by philosophy.

What about privacy?

We don't show your email address. Everything else that you do here - offers, working, commenting - is public for the world to see.

How does payment work?

We use Paypal. You don't need to have an account right away, but you'll need one when you want to send or receive payments, so we suggest you go ahead and create your account.
On the payment page (in Paypal's website) you'll see that 3% of what you're paying is going to FreedomSponsors (we don't mind being transparent).
For example, when you're paying a U$ 10.00 offer, you'll see something like:

  • Mr. Developer ........... U$ 9.70
  • FreedomSponsors ......... U$ 0.30

I don't have a Paypal account, is that ok?

Well, you're gonna need one to send or receive payments.
Your profile has a "primary email" and an optional "Paypal email" field.
If you leave "Paypal email" field blank, and someone tries to send you a payment, FS will consider your primary email as the Paypal account email.
Please note that Paypal will accept that payment *even if the account doesn't exist*. In that case, Paypal will send you an email and you'll have the opportunity to create your account and claim your prize then.
If you intend to send or receive payments, we encourage you to create your Paypal account now.

Two developers are claiming to have resolved my issue. How should I pay?

First you should verify whether the issue is really solved. We encourage sponsors to pay only after their problem is gone!

Then you have to decide whether you'll pay developer A, developer B, or both. To make a fair choice, do a little digging and check out the original issue to see which one(s) of them really worked on it.

For example, maybe developer A submitted a patch for a bugfix, and developer B had to do some rework on it before integrating it into trunk. In that case, they both deserve some credit.
You'll have the option to select how much you pay to whom. The choice is yours.

How a fix done by an independent developer will return back to the main project (trunk)?

Ideally, there should be no "independent developers". The best way for a developer to contribute to an open source project is to actually get involved with the corresponding community.
In most cases, it is also a bad thing for the end user (meaning, the sponsor) if the work is done on a branch, or a fork of the main codebase, because then the user would not benefit from new improvements that eventually get added in the future.

The "Sponsor issue" form has a "No Forking" checkbox as part of the acceptance criteria. By checking it, the sponsor is telling that only solutions that are implemented on the main project's codebase will be accepted.
We want to encourage sponsors to actually fund the original projects, so that checkbox comes checked by default.

I resolved an issue, but the sponsor just didn't pay me. Is there anything I can do?

Well, to actually get paid, no, not really.
But you can add comments to every issue and every offer (offer, and issues are separate things. One issue can have many offers.)
So you can add a comment to that offer and write whatever you want. It will be forever linked to that sponsor's account.
